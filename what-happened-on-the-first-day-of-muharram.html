What happened on the first day of Muharram?

The first day of Muharram marks the start of the Islamic New Year. It brings the official start of the Hijri calendar (you can convert dates anytime using our Bidirectional Date Converter). Historically, it carries deep sorrow for the Muslim community. Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab passed away on this exact day. He died in the year 23 AH after a tragic attack. This day holds heavy historical and spiritual weight for all believers.

The Meaning of the Sacred Month

Muharram is the first month of the Islamic year. It is one of the four sacred months in Islam. The word itself means "forbidden" or "highly respected." Warfare is strictly prohibited during this sacred time. Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) called it the month of Allah. This highlights its immense spiritual value and prestige. Believers use this time for intense spiritual reflection. The month demands peace, fasting, and deep daily prayer.

The Birth of the Hijri Calendar

Let us look at the history of the calendar. The Islamic calendar began under Caliph Umar. It officially started in the year 638 CE. Early Muslims needed a unified way to track time. They did not start the calendar with the Prophet's birth. Instead, they chose the momentous event of the Hijrah. The Hijrah was the migration of early Muslims from Mecca to Medina. This massive migration happened in 622 CE. It was a turning point for the Muslim community. Muharram was chosen as the calendar's starting point. It naturally follows the sacred month of Hajj. This makes First Muharram a time of fresh beginnings.

The Tragic Loss of Caliph Umar

Who died on the first day of this month? The answer is Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab. He was the second rightful caliph of Islam. His leadership shaped early Islamic governance significantly. He was attacked while leading the early dawn prayers. An assassin named Abu Lu'lu struck him cruelly. The attack happened in the final days of Dhul Hijjah. Caliph Umar survived his deep wounds for three days. He finally passed away on the first day of Muharram. This sad event was happened in the end of the year 23 AH. His death brought deep grief to the Muslim world. It marked a major shift in early Islamic history.

Detailed Account of Umar's Assassination

The attack on Caliph Umar was entirely unexpected. Medina was a very safe and peaceful capital city. Umar was leading the early morning Fajr prayer. The grand mosque was full of devout worshippers. Abu Lu'lu hid in the dark corners quietly. He carried a deadly, double-bladed poisoned dagger. As Umar began the prayer, the assassin struck. He stabbed the Caliph multiple times very quickly. The strong poison made the wounds completely fatal. Umar fell down, and another companion continued the prayer. The assassin tried to escape the crowded mosque immediately. He stabbed several other innocent worshippers during his flight. Eventually, he was cornered by the angry crowd. He took his own life with his dagger. Umar was carried to his modest home right away.

Umar's Final Days and Succession

Umar survived for three days after the brutal attack. He used this short time to secure the state. He did not name a direct successor himself. Instead, he formed a special advisory council of six. They were tasked with choosing the next Caliph fairly. Umar wanted a peaceful leadership transition for the people. He asked Aisha (Prophet Muhammad’s wife) for a final, beautiful favor. He wanted to be buried next to his best friends. These friends were Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and Abu Bakr. Aisha graciously granted his deeply heartfelt request. Umar passed away on the first of Muharram. His burial marked the end of a golden era.

Clarifying the History: Day 1 vs. Day 10

Many people confuse the events of this sacred month. We must separate the start of the month from later events. First Muharram marks the new Islamic year. It is the day Caliph Umar passed away. However, it is not the Day of Ashura. Ashura takes place later on the tenth day. The tenth day brings the historic tragedy of Karbala. This is when Imam Hussain was brutally martyred. The Battle of Karbala happened in 61 AH. Hussain ibn Ali (Prophet Muhammad’s grandson) stood bravely against intense political tyranny. His sacrifice on Ashura is remembered globally today (for a deep dive into this event, read What is Ashura and Why is it Celebrated?). But we must remember the historical timeline clearly. Day 1 is entirely distinct from Day 10. Both days hold immense sorrow and profound significance. Yet, they represent very different historical milestones.

The Shadows of Karbala

Though First Muharram is not Ashura, heavy shadows loom. The sadness of the Battle of Karbala begins to settle in. Imam Hussain's small caravan was already moving. They were heading toward a brutal, tragic destiny. He knew the immense risks of his long journey. He refused to pledge loyalty to a corrupt ruler. He chose dangerous truth over safe, quiet compliance. The early days of the month build emotional tension. Believers feel this tension as the month actually starts. The historical grief is truly impossible to ignore.

Historical Reflections on the Year 61 AH

Let us briefly look at the year 61 AH. This was the year of the Karbala tragedy. The Islamic New Year started with heavy, dark tension. Hussain Ibn Ali was fighting for basic Islamic principles. The political climate had become extremely corrupt then. Hussain could not accept this dark, unjust reality. His journey was one of immense moral courage. He knew his small group faced massive state armies. He stepped into the New Year with unmatched bravery. We must draw lasting strength from his noble mindset.

The Significance of the Sacred Month

The sacred month of Muharram commands great religious respect. It is a time for believers to seek true forgiveness. Good deeds are rewarded heavily during this holy period. Sins are also considered much more severe now. Fasting is highly recommended throughout this month. The Prophet (PBUH) encouraged fasting during Muharram often. He said it is the best fast after Ramadan. This elevates the spiritual status of the month greatly. Many devoted Muslims fast on the very first day. They do this to start the year with absolute purity.

Fasting as a Spiritual Shield

Fasting in Muharram acts as a strong spiritual shield. It protects the human soul from worldly distractions. When we fast, we control our most basic desires. This strict control bleeds into our daily moral behavior. We become slower to anger and petty frustration. We speak to others with more kindness and care. The Prophet highly valued fasting in this specific month. We follow his noble example by fasting today. It clears the busy mind for much better worship. It helps us focus entirely on our ultimate purpose.

Preparation for the Ashura Fasts

The first day reminds us to prepare our minds early. We know the Day of Ashura is rapidly approaching. Ashura marks when Moses escaped the cruel Pharaoh. God miraculously parted the Red Sea for the Israelites. The Prophet fasted on Ashura to show immense gratitude. He told his loyal followers to do the exact same. He also planned to fast on the ninth day. This was to distinguish Muslims from other religious groups. First Muharram gives us nine full days to prepare. We should get our bodies ready for this fast.

The Virtues of the Month of Allah

Calling it the "month of Allah" is highly significant. This attribution shows its pure, unmatched spiritual prestige. We must respect what God has elevated so highly. Sinning during this time is a very grave mistake. We must fiercely guard our eyes, ears, and tongues. We must avoid arguments and useless, angry daily debates. True peace must start within our own personal lives.

How Muslims Observe the Islamic New Year

The Islamic New Year is not a loud typical celebration. There are no loud street parties or fireworks. Instead, it is a period of quiet, internal reflection. Believers look back deeply on the previous year. They repent sincerely for their past daily mistakes. They set strong goals for the coming new months. This serious mindset aligns with core Islamic teachings. Life is very short, and time passes quite quickly. The New Year gently reminds us of our own mortality.

Setting Goals for the Islamic Year

A new Islamic year needs fresh, strong spiritual goals. We should write down our personal religious targets. Maybe we want to memorize more of the Quran. Perhaps we need to improve our daily obligatory prayers. We might want to give much more to local charity. First Muharram is the absolute perfect time for planning. We must make these goals realistic and easily measurable. Small, consistent daily actions are better than brief bursts. God loves good deeds that are done continuously. Start small this Muharram and build your habits upward.

The Power of Sincere Repentance

The start of the year invites deep, sincere repentance. We all make mistakes and commit daily hidden sins. God's door of mercy and forgiveness is always wide open. We should use First Muharram to seek immediate pardon. Sincere repentance requires genuine regret deeply in the heart. We must stop the bad habit completely and immediately. We must promise God not to repeat the specific sin. If we hurt someone, we must apologize to them directly. This is exactly how we cleanse our souls completely. Start the new Islamic year with a totally clean slate.

Avoiding Cultural Superstitions

Some people hold entirely wrong beliefs about this month. They falsely think Muharram brings bad luck naturally. This is a highly harmful and completely incorrect superstition. No month in pure Islam is inherently unlucky ever. Muharram is a fully sacred month of God. It is a time of immense blessings and grace. We must completely reject any cultural myths about it. Marriage and happy family events are not strictly forbidden. However, many avoid them out of deep cultural respect. We must separate pure religion from local cultural habits.

Educating the Next Generation

We must teach our young children about this month. They need to fully know the Islamic calendar. They should learn about the Hijrah's deep, historical meaning. Tell them the powerful story of Caliph Umar. Explain the stunning bravery of Hussain ibn Ali to them. These heroic stories build strong character in young minds. Children need real, historical heroes to look up to. Islamic history is completely full of such amazing heroes. First Muharram is a truly great family teaching opportunity. Let us share this vital history openly and proudly.

The Role of Charity and Kindness

First Muharram is a fantastic day for giving charity. Giving wealth purifies our remaining daily income greatly. It brings immense joy to the poor and needy. God multiplies charitable rewards during these specific sacred months. A small donation can carry massive spiritual weight now. We should eagerly feed the hungry in our local communities. We should help orphans and poor, struggling widows financially. These actions reflect the true, beating spirit of Islam. They directly honor the social justice of Umar and Hussain ibn Ali. Good deeds make the Islamic New Year shine brightly.

FAQ

Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab died on this specific day. He passed away from severe assassin wound injuries. He died in the Islamic year 23 AH.

What happened on 1st Muharram in Islam?

Why is Muharram a sad day?

What is forbidden in Muharram?

It is a legally sacred month in Islam. Initiating warfare and combat is strictly forbidden right now. Sinful behavior is considered much worse during this month.
