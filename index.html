Understanding the Hijri and Gregorian Calendars

The Solar vs. Lunar Dynamics

The core difference between these two systems lies in the celestial bodies they track. The Gregorian calendar is universally recognized as the primary civil calendar today. It is a solar calendar based on the Earth's revolution around the sun, consisting of 365 days (or 366 in leap years) divided into 12 months. This system ensures that the months correspond directly with the seasons.

In contrast, the Islamic or Hijri calendar is strictly a lunar calendar based on the phases of the moon. Each month begins with the first sighting of the new crescent moon. A lunar year is approximately 354 or 355 days long, which is roughly 11 days shorter than a solar year. Because of this 11-day deficit, Islamic dates and holidays migrate backwards through all the seasons of the solar year over a 33-year cycle.

How Our Mathematical Converter Works

Our tool utilizes the renowned Tabular Islamic Calendar algorithm anchored by Julian Day Numbers (JDN). This ensures a high level of mathematical accuracy without needing continuous server pings. The system calculates the integer number of days elapsed since the calendar's epoch. For the Hijri calendar, the epoch (1 Muharram 1 AH) aligns with Friday, 16 July 622 CE (Civil Epoch). This precision calculation helps you plan effectively years in advance.
